Tadasana (Mountain Pose)

Tadasana or the Mountain Pose is a basic yoga pose that is very beneficial for our health. Beginners are often asked to practise this yoga pose. Although it is a basic yoga pose and won’t tire you out, practising it regularly can improve your health.

Benefits:

  • It is an ideal pose for those with weak back muscles and rounded backs as it can help restore the body’s natural alignment.
  • This pose helps you connect to your deeper consciousness and expands your mental awareness, making you feel more alert, calm, and composed.
  • Tadasana opens up your lungs and lets you breathe deeply.

Here’s how to do it:

  • Stand on a yoga mat. Your feet should be slightly apart.
  • Your hands should be alongside your body.
  • Inhale deeply and slowly.

Utthita Tadasana (Star Pose)

Star Pose is an active, invigorating yoga pose that energizes the body and improves posture. Its open stance feels expansive, and it is often considered to be a pose that brings joy. It is sometimes called 'Five-Pointed Star Pose.'

Benefits:

  • Strengthens the arms and legs.
  • Expands the lungs.
  • Develops focus and concentration.
  • Improves balance.

Here’s how to do it:

  • Stand on a yoga mat. Your feet should be spread apart.
  • Your hands should also be spread apart.
  • Keep your torso upright.

Utkatasana (Chair Pose)

The chair pose looks deceptively easy and straightforward; yet, when you try it, you’ll find it demands a great deal of flexibility in the shoulders as well as stability in the core and strength in the legs. A chair pose a day will pay.

Benefits:

  • It will strengthen your thighs.
  • It helps to stabilize your knees.
  • Your ankles will become more limber and sturdy.
  • The pose can also help improve your posture.

Here’s how to do it:

  • Stand on a yoga mat. Bend your knees toward a right angle while shifting your weight back into your heels.
  • Hold this pose and then press your palms together in front your chest to do the namaste.
  • Inhale deeply and slowly.

Utkata Konasana (Goddess Pose)

Utkata Konasana or the Goddess Pose is also known as the Fierce Angle Pose. For the lay person or the yogi, Goddess Pose does strengthens the legs and helps train you to get a sense and feeling for a strong back. This is a great place for a long hold and to add a long series of fire breathing technique. The longer you hold Goddess Pose will allow you to judge which joints are weak and tight.

Benefits:

  • Strengthens the hips, the lower back and the thigh muscles.
  • It widens the uterus before pregnancy and helps during pregnancy.
  • It helps to broaden the chest and correct postural defects.
  • It energizes the body, relieves stress and gives a general sense of well-being.
  • It can relieve menstrual pain and cramps.

Here’s how to do it:

  • Start standing, separate your feet approximately distance of length of one of your legs.
  • You can turn your feet out to a comfortable degree, usually 45 degrees is comfortable.
  • Raise your arms out straight from your shoulders and bend them at your elbows and face your palms forward.
  • Start to slowly bend your knees until they also reach a 90 degree angle and hold the pose.
  • Engage your core.

Eka Pada Utkata Konasana (One-Legged Goddess Pose)

Eka Pada Utkata Konasana is a beginner level yoga pose that is performed in standing position. Eka Pada Utkata Konasana additionally involves balance and stretch. This is a great pose to help correct your balance and to add a long series of fire breathing technique.

Benefits:

  • Strengthens the lower back.
  • It widens the uterus before pregnancy and helps during pregnancy.
  • It helps to correct postural defects.
  • It energizes the body, relieves stress and gives a general sense of well-being.

Here’s how to do it:

  • Start standing, lift your right leg to form a 90 degree at your knee.
  • Raise your arms out straight from your shoulders and bend them at your elbows and face your palms forward.
  • Engage your core.
